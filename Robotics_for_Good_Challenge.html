Robotics for Good Youth Challenge 2024-2025

The Robotics for Good Youth Challenge is a global initiative aimed at empowering students to design, build, and program robotic solutions that address critical global challenges, including those related to the United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). The Nigerian edition of this challenge will focus on disaster management, encouraging participants to develop innovative solutions for issues like earthquake response, seismic risk prediction, and environmental resilience.

The competition will bring together 40 teams of students from schools across Nigeria, with each team comprising up to six participants. Three winning teams will represent Nigeria at the AI for Good Global Summit 2025 in Geneva, Switzerland, with all travel expenses covered by ITU.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • All upper primary to senior secondary students are qualified to participate in this competition.
  • All regions in Nigeria can participate.
  • Teams can consist of up to 8 members. Individual participation is also allowed.

Organize a robotics and coding team with five to ten people on your team to participate. A team needs to be supervised by one to three teachers from their school and have permission from the school to compete.

Registration is open annually from December 1st to March 31st. The competition would get in touch with the registered schools.

There would be just 2 Phases which include;
  • National Phases: Competitions would be held in Nigeria.
  • International Phase: Winners of the National Phases would compete for the International Phase which would be held at Geneva, Switzerland.

Its is ABSOLUTELY FREE. No registration fee is required. However all schools and team are register online.

  • National Phase would be held in Nigeria.
  • The Grand Finale would be held at Geneva, Switzerland.

  • National Competition: All winners gets an all expense paid-express ticket to Geneva, Switzerland for the finale.
  • International Competition: Winner would win prizes for the school and for the members of the winning team.
